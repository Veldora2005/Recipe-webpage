Chicken Biryani

Indian . Serves:4 . 1.5Hrs

Indian . Serves:4
1.5Hrs

Ingridents

  • 1 large whole chicken
  • 1½ cups basmati rice
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 3 tsp Masala or chicken masala
  • ¾ cup yogurt
  • ½ tbsp ginger
  • ½ tbsp garlic
  • 1 handful coriander leaves and for garnish
  • 1 handful mint leaves and for garnish
  • 2-3 green chillies
  • 2 large brown onions
  • 4-5 cardamoms
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ½ inch cinnamon stick
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 medium tomato
  • A large pinch of saffron
  • ¼ cup milk
  • Oil for frying onions
  • Salt

Raita Ingridents

  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • A few mint leaves
  • ¼ cup chopped cilantro
  • ½ cup chopped cucumber
  • ½ cup chopped tomato
  • ¼ cup chopped red onion
  • ½ tsp cumin powder
  • ½ tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp salt

Procedure

Step 1

  • Peel and finely slice the onion (use a mandolin or knife).
  • In a deep pan, add frying oil and bring to 135°C – 275°F.
  • Fry a batch at a time until brown.

Step 2

  • In a dry pan, add 1 tsp chilli powder, 3 tsp Masala, 1 tsp coriander powder, ½ tsp turmeric powder.
  • Toast until fragrant.
  • Leave to cool.

Step 3

  • In a mixing bowl, add yogurt, toasted spices, 1 tsp salt, ginger paste, garlic paste.
  • Add chopped coriander, green chillies, mint leaves, and half the fried onions.
  • Add deconstructed chicken and mix well.
  • Set aside for 2–24 hours.

Step 4

  • In a deep bottom pan, add water with 4-5 cardamoms, bay leaf, cinnamon stick, and salt.
  • Bring water to a boil.
  • Add rice and cook al dente (about three-quarters done).
  • Drain rice and set aside.

Step 5

  • In a deep pan, heat 1 tbsp ghee.
  • Add marinated chicken and tomato.
  • Add 1 tsp Masala.
  • Layer three-quarters of the cooked rice evenly on top.
  • Sprinkle remaining fried onions, chopped coriander, and 7–8 torn mint leaves on top.

Step 6

  • Add milk-soaked saffron.
  • Drizzle 1 tbsp remaining ghee.
  • Sprinkle salt and remaining Masala powder.

Step 7

  • Cover and cook for 40 minutes.
  • Remove from heat.

Step 8

  • Garnish with chopped coriander, mint leaves, and remaining fried onions.
  • Serve hot.

Raita Steps

  • Whisk the yogurt in a mixing bowl until smooth and creamy.
  • Add chopped cucumber, tomato, red onion, and cilantro to the bowl and stir thoroughly.
  • Sprinkle in cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt, then mix well to combine.
  • Taste the raita and adjust seasoning by adding more salt or spices if necessary.
  • Serve immediately or cover and refrigerate until ready to serve.
  • Optionally, personalize with grated carrots, chopped mint, or other vegetables/herbs.
  • Enjoy your refreshing raita!

Notes

  • Use a heavy bottom pan
  • Put on Dum On Low Heat
  • Ensure proper sealing before putting on Dum
  • Recipe Credit:- Recipe30

Garam Masala Grilled Chicken

Indian . Serves:4 . 2.5Hrs

Indian . Serves:4
2.5Hrs

Ingridents

  • 14oz – 400g boneless chicken thighs (skinless)
  • ¾ cup plain or Greek yoghurt
  • 1 tbsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely grated
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp sea salt

Cucumber Salad Ingridents

  • 1 large cucumber, finely sliced
  • ½ small red onion, finely sliced
  • ¼ cup fresh dill or mint leaves
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Pinch of sea salt

Yoghurt Tahini Sauce Ingridents

  • ¾ cup plain or Greek yoghurt
  • 1 tbsp tahini
  • 1 garlic clove, finely grated
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp water (to loosen, if needed)
  • Pinch of sea salt

Procedure

Step 1

  • In a mixing bowl, add plain or Greek yoghurt, garam masala, cumin, smoked paprika, grated garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, olive oil, and sea salt. Mix until smooth.

Step 2

  • Add the chicken thighs to the marinade, coat well using clean hands or tongs. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best flavour.

Step 3

  • Preheat a grill or griddle pan over medium-high heat.

Step 4

  • Grill the marinated chicken thighs for 5 to 6 minutes on each side, or until lightly charred and fully cooked through.

Step 5

  • Remove from the heat and rest the chicken for a few minutes before slicing.

Step 6

  • Serve warm with a fresh cucumber, red onion, and mint salad, or wrap in flatbread with a drizzle of tahini or yoghurt sauce.

Cucumber Salad Steps

  • In a mixing bowl, combine sliced cucumber, red onion, and dill or mint leaves.
  • Add lemon juice, olive oil, and sea salt. Toss gently and set aside to marinate.
  • Serve the salad chilled alongside the grilled chicken, or use it in a warm flatbread wrap.

Yoghurt Tahini Sauce Steps

  • In a mixing bowl, add yoghurt, tahini, grated garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, and sea salt.
  • Mix until smooth. Add a splash of water if the sauce is too thick.
  • Serve with grilled chicken or as a drizzle for wraps.

Notes

  • If you're short on time, 30 minutes of marinating will still give decent flavour — just increase the spice a little.

Chana Masala

Indian . Serves:2 . 30Mins

Indian . Serves:2
30Mins

Ingridents

  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 (1-inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, chopped (or more to taste)
  • 1 green chile pepper, seeded and chopped (Optional)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 fresh bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 pinch salt, to taste
  • Water, as needed
  • 1 (15 ounce) can chickpeas
  • 1 teaspoon fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish (or more to taste)

Procedure

Step 1

  • Grind onion, tomato, ginger, garlic, and chile pepper together in a food processor into a paste.

Step 2

  • Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  • Fry bay leaves in hot oil until fragrant (about 30 seconds).

Step 3

  • Pour the paste into the skillet and cook until the oil begins to separate and mixture turns golden brown (2–3 minutes).

Step 4

  • Season the mixture with chili powder, coriander, garam masala, turmeric, and salt.
  • Cook and stir until very hot (2–3 minutes).

Step 5

  • Stir just enough water into the mixture to get a thick sauce.
  • Bring to a boil.

Step 6

  • Stir chickpeas into the sauce.
  • Reduce heat to medium and cook until chickpeas are heated through (5–7 minutes).

Step 7

  • Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Notes

  • For Best experiance serve with Naan

Butter Chicken

Indian . Serves:4 . 1.5Hrs

Indian . Serves:4
1.5Hrs

Ingridents

  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 2 teaspoons tandoori masala powder
  • 2 teaspoons Madras curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon ground cardamom
  • ½ teaspoon ground cayenne pepper
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 ½ pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-size pieces
  • 3 tablespoons butter, divided
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons chopped fresh ginger
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1 cup half-and-half
  • ¼ cup plain yogurt
  • ⅓ cup cashews
  • 1 bunch fresh cilantro, stems removed

Procedure

Step 1

  • Make a spice mix by combining garam masala, tandoori masala, curry powder, cumin, cardamom, cayenne, salt, and black pepper in a small bowl.
  • Set aside.

Step 2

  • Place chicken in a large bowl and add half of the spice mixture.
  • Turn to coat evenly.

Step 3

  • Melt 1 tablespoon butter in a large skillet over medium heat.
  • Add chicken and cook, stirring, until lightly browned (about 10 minutes).
  • Remove from heat and set aside.

Step 4

  • Melt remaining 2 tablespoons butter in a large saucepan over medium heat.
  • Add onion and cook until soft and translucent (about 5 minutes).
  • Stir in remaining spice mixture, garlic, lemon juice, and ginger; cook until combined (about 1 minute).

Step 5

  • Stir tomato puree into onion mixture and cook, stirring frequently, about 2 minutes.
  • Pour in half-and-half and yogurt.
  • Reduce heat to low and simmer, stirring frequently, about 10 minutes.
  • Remove from heat.

Step 6

  • Blend cashews in a blender until finely ground.
  • Add sauce to the blender and puree until smooth.

Step 7

  • Pour blended sauce over chicken in the skillet.
  • Simmer until thickened, 10 to 15 minutes.
  • Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Notes

  • If you have trouble finding tandoori masala, it is a blend of paprika, cumin, coriander, garlic, ginger, and cardamom.
  • Recipe Credit:- Allrecipes

Bread Pakoda

Indian . Serves:4 . 30Mins

Indian . Serves:4
30Mins

Ingridents

  • 4 slices of white bread
  • Oil for deep frying

For the Batter

  • 150 g gram flour (chickpea flour)
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
  • Salt to taste

For the Potato Mix

  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 600 g potatoes, boiled and roughly crushed
  • Pinch of asafoetida
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 7–10 curry leaves
  • 1 birds eye green chilli, finely chopped
  • 2 tsp finely chopped ginger
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 60 g frozen green peas
  • 1 tbsp roughly chopped coriander
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Salt to taste

Procedure

Step 1

  • Heat oil in a heavy bottom sauce pan. Add the asafoetida followed by mustard seeds.
  • As they splutter add the curry leaves and green chillies.
  • Fry for a minute on medium heat and add the chopped ginger. Fry for a further minute.

Step 2

  • Add turmeric powder; stir and add the potatoes along with the green peas.
  • Stir to make sure the mix is evenly coloured.
  • Cover and steam on a low heat for 3–4 minutes.
  • Garnish with coriander, add lemon juice and season to taste.
  • Let the mix cool while you make the gram flour batter.

Step 3

  • In a large mixing bowl add the gram flour along with the powdered spices and salt.
  • Add water and whisk well to make a smooth batter.
  • Ensure the batter has a double cream consistency.

Step 4

  • Place the sliced bread on a plate and add the spiced potato mix.
  • Flatten the potato mix with the back of a spoon.
  • Place another slice over it to make a sandwich.
  • Cut the sandwich diagonally.
  • Repeat for the rest of the potato mix.

Step 5

  • Heat oil in a wok or large kadhai for deep frying.
  • Dip the potato sandwich in the gram flour batter mix.
  • Deep fry until light brown and crisp, about 1–2 minutes on each side.
  • Drain on kitchen paper.
  • Cut the bread pakoda into smaller pieces and serve warm with chutney.

Notes

  • The potato mix is easy to make, though the potato is best crushed by hand as you want a slightly lumpy mix rather than smooth mashed potato base.

Mughlai Malai Kofta

Indian . Serves:4 . 2Hrs

Indian . Serves:4
2Hrs

Ingridents

  • 300gms potatoes, boiled
  • 200gms paneer, finely grated
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground cardamom powder
  • 1” piece of ginger, finely grated
  • 1 birds eye green chilli, finely chopped
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 tbsp coriander leaves, finely chopped
  • 3 tsp cornflour
  • 2 tbsp raisins
  • Oil for deep frying

Ingredients for Gravy

  • 6 cloves of garlic, roughly chopped
  • 1” ginger, roughly chopped
  • 1 birds eye green chilli
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 6 cloves
  • 1” cinnamon stick, halved
  • 1 green chilli, slit lengthwise
  • 120gms white onion, roughly chopped
  • 200gms tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 2 tbsp tomato puree
  • ½ tsp chilli powder (mild or Kashmiri chilli powder)
  • 80gms cashew nuts
  • 200mls water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ½ tsp garam masala powder
  • 2 tsp mango powder (amchoor)
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander for garnish

Procedure

Step 1

  • Add the cashew nuts to a bowl with 50mls warm water and soften for 30 minutes.
  • Blend the garlic, ginger and chilli with a splash of water to a smooth paste. Set aside.

Step 2

  • Add the onion to a blender and blend to a smooth fine paste with 50mls of water. Set aside.
  • In the same blender, add the tomatoes and blend to a fine puree. Set aside.

Step 3

  • Blend the soaked cashew nuts with some of the soaking liquid until smooth and creamy. Set aside.

Step 4

  • For the kofta: Coarsely grate the potatoes and mash well to a smooth mix.
  • Add all the kofta ingredients (except oil) to a large bowl.
  • Knead lightly to a dough-like consistency.
  • Cover with cling film until ready to fry.

Step 5

  • Heat oil in a heavy-bottom saucepan.
  • Add the cloves and cinnamon stick, fry for a few seconds.
  • Add the slit green chilli followed by the onion paste.
  • Fry on medium heat for 5 minutes, stirring to prevent sticking.

Step 6

  • Add the ginger-garlic-chilli paste and fry for 2 minutes.
  • Stir and add blended tomatoes along with the tomato puree.
  • Mix and cook for 6–7 minutes until the sauce reduces and deepens in colour.

Step 7

  • Add chilli powder and cashew nut paste.
  • Stir well and cook for 2 minutes.
  • Lower the heat and add water.
  • Simmer for 2–3 minutes.

Step 8

  • Add sugar, garam masala, and mango powder.
  • Season to taste and garnish with coriander.
  • Turn off the heat and keep the gravy warm.

Step 9

  • Divide the kofta mix into equal portions.
  • Roll each portion into cylindrical shapes.
  • Ensure they are shaped well to avoid breaking while frying.

Step 10

  • Fry koftas in small batches (2–3 at a time) for 3–4 minutes.
  • Cook until crispy golden brown.
  • Drain on kitchen paper.

Notes

  • Add the warm gravy to a serving dish and steep the koftas just before serving. Accompanied rice, salad and raita.
  • Recipe Credit:- Maunika Gowardan

Punjabi Aloo Gobi

Indian . Serves:4 . 45Mins

Indian . Serves:4
45Mins

Ingridents

  • 480gms cauliflower (cut into small florets)
  • 260gms potatoes (boiled, peeled, and diced to bite-size pieces)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Pinch of hing (asafoetida)
  • 1 heaped tsp cumin seeds
  • ½” ginger (blended to a paste with a splash of water)
  • 3 green chillies (slit lengthwise)
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 ½ tbsp coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Handful of coriander (finely chopped, for garnish)
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • 1 tbsp dried fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi, optional)
  • ½” ginger (cut into slivers, for garnish)

Procedure

  • Heat oil in a non-stick heavy-bottom saucepan over medium flame.
  • Add hing (asafoetida) and cumin seeds. Fry for 1 minute.
  • Add ginger paste and slit green chillies. Fry for 20 seconds.
  • Add turmeric powder and coriander powder. Fry for a few seconds.
  • Add cauliflower florets, season with salt, and stir well for 1 minute so florets are coated with spices.
  • Add 50ml water, stir, reduce heat to low, cover with a lid, and cook for 15–17 minutes (stir halfway).
  • Add boiled potatoes and mix well.
  • Garnish with coriander, lemon juice, kasoori methi, and ginger slivers. Stir and turn off the heat.
  • Keep covered for a few minutes, then serve warm with chapattis and raita.

Notes

  • This is a dry dish and perfect to scoop up with chapattis or naan along with raita.
  • Recipe Credit:- Maunika Gowardan

Kesar Ras Malai

Indian . Serves:4 . 3Hrs

Indian . Serves:4
3Hrs

Ingridents

For the Thickened Milk

  • 800mls whole milk
  • 200gms condensed milk
  • 100gms caster sugar
  • ¼ tsp cardamom powder
  • A good pinch of saffron

For the Milk Dumplings / Paneer

  • 2 pints whole milk
  • Juice of 2 lemons

For the Syrup

  • 200gms caster sugar
  • 400mls water
  • Pinch of saffron

For Garnish

  • Chopped pistachio
  • Silver varkh

Procedure

Step 1: Prepare the Milk Sauce

  • In a heavy bottom saucepan, add all the milk sauce ingredients.
  • Bring to a gentle boil and cook for about 1 hour 30 minutes until reduced by half and slightly thick.
  • Turn off the heat, cool, and refrigerate until the dumplings are ready.

Step 2: Make the Paneer

  • In a heavy bottom non-stick saucepan, bring the whole milk to a gentle boil.
  • Add lemon juice and boil for 30 seconds until it curdles.
  • Remove from heat and strain through muslin.

Step 3: Rinse & Drain

  • Rinse the curdled mix under cold water to remove sourness and help it come together.
  • Keep it in muslin on a sieve for 1 hour to drain excess water.

Step 4: Prepare the Syrup

  • In a separate pan, combine sugar, water, and saffron.
  • Bring to a boil and keep it ready for cooking the dumplings.

Step 5: Shape the Dumplings

  • Knead the drained paneer until smooth.
  • Divide into 6 equal portions and flatten into small discs.

Step 6: Cook in Syrup

  • Place the discs gently into the hot syrup.
  • Cook for 7–10 minutes until they become firm.
  • Remove and let them cool slightly.

Step 7: Soak in Milk Sauce

  • Transfer the cooked dumplings into the chilled thickened milk sauce.
  • Let them soak for 4–5 hours in the refrigerator.

Step 8: Garnish & Serve

  • Place the dumplings with the milk sauce in bowls.
  • Garnish with chopped pistachios and silver varkh before serving.

Notes

  • The dumplings are made with curdling milk solids, though if you prefer to omit the step a good option would be the use of ricotta cheese instead.
  • Recipe Credit:- Maunika Gowardan
